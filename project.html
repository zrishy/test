Introduction

Rhythm gaming has always been a part of my life, starting from Guitar Hero and currently it has evolved into where I am now, I play multiple games competetively for teams and myself.

My Inquiry Question. How does Rhythm Gaming teach new users about competition?

I have most of my rhythm gaming experience due to playing osu! for five years, it has been with me for nearly a third of my life. osu! has taught me very valuable things in life that have made sports, my outlook on the world, and knowledge improve.

osu! is a rhythm game primarily developed, published and created by Dean "peppy" Herbert. Originally released for Microsoft Windows on September 16, 2007, the game has also been ported to macOS. Its gameplay is based on titles including Osu! Tatakae! Ouendan, Taiko no Tatsujin, Beatmania IIDX, Elite Beat Agents, O2Jam, and DJMax. The game has an active e-sports community, and is also sometimes recommended by professional players of other games to practice or warm-up.

Personally, some of the things I have learned from playing these games for extended periods of time, is that moderation and persistence are key to becoming better. Without moderation, persistence, and the knowledge I have gained over the years, where I am now could've never been possible.

Strategies

The strategies listed below can be applied to real life scenarios, and other games if you tried hard enough.

1. Everyday will not always give the same performance. You have good days and bad days, without bad days you can never have good days. But really, any day can be a good day.

2. If you put in more work than everyone else, you will eventually surpass those which you were competing against.

3. Everything you do should have a goal, there is no point in doing anything if you dont have some sort of an end goal, planning what you need to do to achieve this goal is key.

4. Pushing yourself hard is a very good practice, but taking it too far can injure yourself contributing negative velocity towards you getting better at the sport in which you practice this strategy. Always practice moderation with any of these strategies!

Using these four strategies have proven to be worth it for me and my teammates! With enough work you would really be suprised what you are capable of.
